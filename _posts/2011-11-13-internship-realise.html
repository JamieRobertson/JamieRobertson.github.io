Between October and November I was lucky enough to be given a month long internship at 'Realise' digital agency in Edinburgh.

I worked on live projects for a variety of clients. I was creating client sites, doing competitor analysis concerning social networking, creating avatars and other icons, producing short augmented realities with After Effects and making Flash adverts.

The experience taught me a lot about working in a professional atmosphere, like the fast pace of production and edits.

I also learnt how to use flash tracking technologies like MediaMind (Eyeblaster) and DoubleClick, and about using Flash plugins such as greenSock, to speed up the creation of ads.

I was also asked to think of concepts for an aurasma. Aurasma is an augmented reality platform. When you see a print advert like the one below, you could hover your smart phone or tablet over the advert and once the device recognizes the image, it would play a short video commercial, possibly with a link at the end to find out more.

I used Adobe After Effects to create the short mock-up below, it was the third or fourth time I had used After Effects and really enjoyed the software, I hope I can use it much more in the future. (note: this work is draft quality, was done on spec and should be treated as a prototype).
