In the eight weeks leading up to the Notting Hill Carnival, Franky Athill and I designed and built our first native phone app- the 'Notting Hill Carnival App'.

Design:

The app consisted of a home screen with six interactive maps, one Twitter news feed and one static offline map. Each interactive map provided information on a different element of the carnival:

- transportation - the nearest tube stations and their current status
- the parade route - including the judging area
- first aid points
- food stalls
- sound systems - including a drop down list
- toilet facilities

 

 

Development:

The app was developed using the PhoneGap framework and used the Google Maps JavaScript API. This meant that the whole app could be written with HTML5 and CSS and all the programming could be done using JavaScript, jQuery and JSON. Using the PhoneGap framework meant that I could take advantage of the phone's native GPS functionality.

Accompanying Website:

We also created a dedicated website for the app, www.nottinghillcarnival.eu, which provides useful information about the carnival, interesting and relevant blog posts and directs users to the Google & Apple app stores.
